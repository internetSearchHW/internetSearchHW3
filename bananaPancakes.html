FLUFFY Banana Pancakes Recipe

FLUFFY Banana Pancakes

This is a recipe for the Fluffiest banana pancakes you will ever taste!

Servings: 6 pancakes

Course: Breakfast

Cuisine: Gluten-Free, Grain-Free

Prep time: 7 minutes

Cook time: 10 minutes

Total time: 17 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium ripe bananas (yellow with brown spots)
  • 2 tsp baking powder (not necessary, but helps them rise)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (or citrus zest or sub another extract of choice)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 4-6 Tbsp coconut flour (or sub almond flour)
  • Oil (for cooking)

Toppings: It is up to you! - Berries, Fruit, Seeds, Yogurt, Maple syrup

Instructions

  1. To a large mixing bowl, add bananas and mash until only small bits remain.
  2. Then add baking powder and vanilla extract and use a fork or whisk to mash until thoroughly combined.
  3. Next add eggs, break yolks with a fork or whisk, and whisk thoroughly until well combined.
  4. Lastly, add coconut flour 1 Tbsp (9 g) at a time until a thick but scoopable batter is achieved (thicker than your average pancake batter, but not so thick that it appears dry). If you add too much coconut flour, add a little dairy-free milk to thin.
  5. Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add a little cooking oil to coat the pan.
  6. Then spoon in roughly 3-Tbsp amounts of batter and reduce heat to low. These benefit from cooking slower and lower than your average pancakes. Cover with a lid to help the center cook through.
  7. Cook for 3-4 minutes, then remove lid and flip carefully. Cook for 3-4 minutes more (lowering heat as needed if cooking too fast) or until the underside is golden brown. Continue cooking until all batter is used up — as recipe is written, ~6 pancakes.
  8. To serve, top with desired toppings, such as nut butter, sliced bananas or fresh fruit, dairy-free yogurt, or maple syrup. We also love a sprinkle of seeds for more fiber and healthy fats.
  9. Enjoy!

Notes

  • You can easly change the recipe, just remember - one banana per one egg!
  • Best when fresh, though leftovers keep covered in the refrigerator 3-4 days, or in the freezer up to 1 month. To reheat, microwaving is most effective. Otherwise, toast or bake in a 180 C (350 degree F) oven until hot.

Nutrition

Serving: 1 pancake Calories: 104 Carbohydrates: 12.4 g Protein: 3.2 g Fat: 4.8 g
